## The Collapse of Old Borders

For decades, the internet operated under the illusion of being a borderless space, when in reality it was anchored to the physical jurisdictions of the 20th century. Geographic domains (ccTLDs) were distributed according to political maps drawn after world wars, not according to cognitive or technological affinities.

Today, that model is obsolete. The United Nations and ICANN manage a paper territory while true global governance shifts to server clusters, foundational language models (LLMs), and blockchains. Code is the new law, and algorithms are the new borders.

In this context, Europe's dependence on foreign technological monopolies is not just an economic risk; it is a **loss of cognitive sovereignty**.
